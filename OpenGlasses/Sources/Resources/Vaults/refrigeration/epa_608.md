# EPA Section 608 — Field Reference

Note: This is a field-reference summary, not legal advice. When in doubt, refer to the current EPA Section 608 regulations directly.

## Who must comply

In the United States, any technician who maintains, services, repairs, or disposes of equipment that could release refrigerant into the atmosphere must hold a current EPA Section 608 certification. Certification level (Type I, II, III, or Universal) must match the equipment.

| Type | Equipment |
|------|-----------|
| Type I | Small appliances (≤ 5 lb refrigerant) |
| Type II | High-pressure systems (commercial split, rooftop, R-410A, R-32) |
| Type III | Low-pressure systems (chillers using R-123, R-11) |
| Universal | All of the above |

Most commercial HVAC field service requires at least Type II.

## Recovery requirements

Refrigerant must be recovered before opening any portion of a refrigerant circuit for service or component replacement. Allowable evacuation levels depend on equipment type and size — common targets:

| Equipment | Required vacuum (inches Hg) |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| HCFC ≤ 200 lb capacity | 0 in Hg (atmospheric) |
| HCFC > 200 lb capacity | 10 in Hg vacuum |
| HFC high-pressure, ≤ 200 lb | 0 in Hg |
| HFC high-pressure, > 200 lb | 10 in Hg vacuum |
| HFC R-410A/R-32 typical commercial | 0 in Hg |

Use only EPA-certified recovery equipment. Recovery cylinders must be DOT-approved and labelled with the recovered refrigerant type and certified empty weight.

## Leak repair triggers

When annualized leak rate exceeds:

- **30%** for commercial refrigeration with full charge > 50 lb
- **20%** for industrial process refrigeration with full charge > 50 lb
- **10%** for comfort cooling with full charge > 50 lb

…the owner must repair leaks within 30 days and verify the repair, or develop a 1-year retrofit/retirement plan.

## Recordkeeping

Maintain records of:

- Refrigerant added or recovered (date, quantity, refrigerant type, equipment serial).
- Leak inspections and repair attempts.
- Disposal manifests.

Retain records for at least 3 years.

## Field-service checklist before opening a circuit

1. Confirm refrigerant type from nameplate.
2. Confirm tech certification level covers this refrigerant class.
3. Confirm recovery machine + cylinder are appropriate type and within annual inspection.
4. Recover refrigerant to required evacuation level.
5. Verify recovery weight against recovered cylinder weight.
6. Log recovered quantity to the work order.
